## Summary

Michelle J Raymond, founder of B2B Growth Co and a specialist in LinkedIn Company Page strategy, is joined by marketing strategist Bec Chappell to deliver a structured "Stop / Start / Rethink / Redo" framework for B2B marketers planning their 2026 LinkedIn strategy. The episode (released both as a podcast episode of Social Media for B2B Growth and as a YouTube video) targets B2B marketers, founders, and leaders who are reassessing what worked in 2025 and what needs to change.

The central argument is a corrective against three specific failure patterns the hosts observed across B2B brands in 2025: over-reliance on AI to generate content, the proliferation of "beige," undifferentiated content that looks identical across competitors, and an unhealthy obsession with chasing every algorithm tweak instead of building durable marketing fundamentals. Chappell's framing of the AI problem is central to the episode: when companies outsource strategic thinking to AI tools, they get "same-same" output that erases the nuance and point of view that would otherwise differentiate them from competitors.

Raymond and Chappell argue that LinkedIn strategy needs to be re-anchored in core marketing fundamentals — brand consistency, audience relevance, and a clear understanding of where LinkedIn fits in the broader marketing mix — rather than being treated as an isolated tactical channel that responds primarily to algorithm signals. The discussion explicitly separates "what to stop" (chasing algorithm changes, leaning on AI shortcuts, producing generic content) from "what to start" (clearer differentiation, stronger brand-led positioning, and content built around genuine relevance to a defined audience).

The episode closes with a forward-looking framing: that the B2B brands who "win" on LinkedIn in 2026 will not be the loudest or most frequent posters, but the ones who pause to think strategically before executing, prioritizing clarity over noise.

## Key Ideas

- Outsourcing content strategy to AI tools produces "same-same" content that erases the differentiation a brand needs to stand out — AI should accelerate execution, not replace strategic thinking
- "Beige," undifferentiated content is a primary failure pattern across B2B brands; creative, opinionated content outperforms generic, safe content
- Chasing every algorithm change is a losing strategy; durable marketing fundamentals (brand, relevance, audience understanding) outperform reactive tactical chasing
- LinkedIn strategy should be evaluated as part of a brand's wider marketing mix, not as an isolated channel with its own disconnected rules
- The brands that win in 2026 will be the ones who slow down to think clearly, not the ones who post the most or react the fastest to platform changes

## Why It Matters for B2B SaaS

This episode functions as a useful counterbalance to the more tactical, algorithm-focused content elsewhere in this research collection (e.g., van der Blom's data-driven reports). Raymond and Chappell's argument is that B2B SaaS marketing teams who treat LinkedIn purely as an algorithm-optimization problem will systematically underperform teams who treat it as a brand and positioning problem first, with algorithm awareness as a secondary layer.

For SaaS marketing leaders specifically, the AI content warning is timely: as more SaaS companies adopt AI writing tools for LinkedIn content at scale, this episode provides a framework for auditing whether that content still carries a defensible point of view, or whether it has converged toward the same generic "thought leadership" tone as every competitor's AI-assisted content.
