## Overview

Melissa Gaglione is a B2B marketing strategist and founder of Citrine, a company that helps organizations build employee-generated content programs. She is known for studying high-performing LinkedIn content, analyzing platform trends, and helping companies activate employees as authentic content creators. Her work focuses on scaling organic reach through employee voices rather than relying exclusively on corporate brand accounts.

## Post 1

**Title:** Reach Is Down 40–50% for Almost Everyone

**Content Type:** LinkedIn Post

**Date:** April 2026

**Source:** [LinkedIn Profile](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/melissagaglione_reach-is-down-40-50-for-almost-everyone-share-7442245666875068419-TiU_/)

### Summary

Melissa discusses declining organic reach across LinkedIn and explains how creators and companies should respond. She argues that algorithm changes are placing greater emphasis on relevance, audience engagement quality, and expertise rather than simply rewarding frequent posting. The post encourages marketers to focus on stronger content quality and audience alignment instead of increasing publishing volume.

### Main Marketing Lesson

As organic reach becomes more competitive, relevance and expertise matter more than posting frequency.

### Why It Matters for B2B SaaS

Many SaaS companies attempt to solve declining reach by publishing more content. Melissa's analysis suggests that improving content quality and audience relevance is often more effective than increasing output.

## Post 2

**Title:** We Need to Stop Complicating B2B Employee-Generated Content

**Content Type:** LinkedIn Post

**Date:** February 2025

**Source:** [LinkedIn Profile](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/melissagaglione_we-need-to-stop-complicating-b2b-employee-generated-ugcPost-7298764458040971264-b75B/)

### Summary

Melissa argues that employee-generated content programs are frequently overengineered. Instead of creating complicated approval systems and content requirements, organizations should help employees share authentic experiences, lessons, and expertise. She explains that authenticity often outperforms highly polished corporate messaging.

### Main Marketing Lesson

Successful employee-generated content programs depend on authenticity, not excessive process.

### Why It Matters for B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS buyers increasingly trust individuals more than company pages. Employee-generated content allows organizations to expand reach and credibility through multiple trusted voices.

## Post 3

**Title:** Your B2B Team Doesn't Have a Content Problem

**Content Type:** LinkedIn Post

**Date:** February 2026

**Source:** [LinkedIn Profile](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/melissagaglione_your-b2b-team-doesnt-have-a-content-problem-share-7426425747721854976--7Gu/)

### Summary

Melissa challenges the idea that companies lack content ideas. Instead, she argues that most organizations already possess valuable insights within their sales, customer success, product, and leadership teams. The real challenge is creating systems that capture and distribute that knowledge consistently.

### Main Marketing Lesson

Most organizations already have enough expertise to create content; they need better systems for extracting and sharing it.

### Why It Matters for B2B SaaS

SaaS companies often struggle to maintain content production. Melissa's framework encourages teams to view internal expertise as a renewable content source, reducing dependence on a small marketing team.
