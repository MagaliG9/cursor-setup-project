## Overview

Dave Gerhardt is one of the most influential voices in modern B2B marketing. As the former CMO of Drift and founder of Exit Five, he has helped popularize founder-led content, community-driven growth, and audience-first marketing. His content focuses on building trust, creating demand through education, and developing long-term relationships with customers rather than relying solely on traditional lead-generation tactics.

## Post 1

**Title:** LinkedIn Is the Number One Channel for B2B

**Content Type:** LinkedIn Post

**Date:** April 2026

**Source:** [LinkedIn Profile](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_linkedin-is-the-number-one-channel-for-b2b-share-7439654088146989059-uZo6/)

### Summary

Dave argues that LinkedIn has become the most important organic channel for B2B marketers because it combines audience targeting, professional context, and content distribution in a single platform. He explains that buyers increasingly rely on trusted individuals and educational content when researching solutions, making LinkedIn an ideal environment for demand creation.

### Main Marketing Lesson

B2B companies should prioritize LinkedIn as a primary channel for building trust, educating buyers, and generating demand.

### Why It Matters for B2B SaaS

Many SaaS companies struggle with rising acquisition costs through paid advertising. LinkedIn provides an opportunity to build organic visibility and establish credibility through consistent thought leadership and audience engagement.

## Post 2

**Title:** Content Marketers Run Modern Marketing

**Content Type:** LinkedIn Post

**Date:** February 2025

**Source:** [LinkedIn Profile](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_content-marketers-run-modern-marketing-ugcPost-7417538553477464064-zUSj/)

### Summary

Dave discusses the growing importance of content marketing within modern go-to-market strategies. He argues that content is no longer a supporting function but a central driver of brand awareness, audience development, and pipeline generation. Effective content marketers help companies create demand by educating prospects and building trust before a sales conversation begins.

### Main Marketing Lesson

Content should be viewed as a strategic business function rather than a tactical marketing activity.

### Why It Matters for B2B SaaS

SaaS buyers often conduct extensive research before engaging with sales teams. Educational content helps companies influence purchasing decisions earlier in the buyer journey and build stronger brand recognition.

## Post 3

**Title:** What Is Good Content? How Do You Measure It?

**Content Type:** LinkedIn Post

**Date:** 2026

**Source:** [LinkedIn Profile](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_what-is-good-content-how-do-you-measure-share-7313541800005099521-44-d/)

### Summary

Dave explores how marketers should evaluate content performance beyond traditional engagement metrics. He emphasizes measuring outcomes such as audience trust, brand awareness, customer conversations, and business impact. The post encourages marketers to think critically about what success actually means rather than focusing solely on likes and impressions.

### Main Marketing Lesson

Content performance should be measured according to business outcomes rather than vanity metrics.

### Why It Matters for B2B SaaS

Many SaaS companies focus on short-term engagement numbers that do not correlate with revenue. Dave's framework encourages marketers to connect content efforts to broader business goals such as demand generation, customer education, and brand authority.
